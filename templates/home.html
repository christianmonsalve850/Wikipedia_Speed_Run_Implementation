Welcome to my Wikipedia Speed Run Implementation!

What it does:

This algorithm finds concise, human-like routes from one Wikipedia article to another by combining semantic sentence embeddings with a heuristic best-first search. Instead of relying solely on exact link text or exhaustive exploration, it ranks candidate pages by how semantically close they are to the target and expands the most promising paths first.

How it works (brief):

  • Represent pages using sentence embeddings that capture meaning, not just keywords.
  • Use a cosine similarity-based heuristic to estimate how close a page is to the target.
  • Run an A*-style best-first search augmented with beam search to focus on the most promising expansions.
  • Apply light penalties for highly popular pages and occasional random exploration to avoid local traps.
  • Stop when a short path is found or configurable time/depth limits are reached.

Benefits:

  • Produces semantically sensible, short paths (more human-like navigation)
  • Efficient—focuses search on promising directions rather than brute force
  • Robust—balances exploitation with exploration to discover creative routes

Technologies used:

  • Python
  • wikipedia-api for link traversal
  • sentence-transformers (Modern BERT-based embedding model)
  • NumPy for vector operations
